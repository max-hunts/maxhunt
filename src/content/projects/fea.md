## Overview

As part of my engineering studies I learned to use Computer Aided Engineering
(Finite Element Analysis) to simulate structural events on model parts. This
helped determine how strong a part needed to be — and in which material — to
satisfy the design requirements with an acceptable safety factor.

The process involved modelling a part in SolidWorks, generating a mesh, and
simulating it under unit or cyclic loads. An important step was iterative mesh
refinement: a draft mesh gives a rough answer, then refinement converges on a
reliable result.
